A PAKHTUN MEMORY

“This is not a place to celebrate happiness.”
(December 28th, 2011 / Shirin Jinnah Colony, Karachi)

In this urban choreography, the Tentative Collective performed rural Pakhtun folk music in a dense urban setting populated by Pakhtun migrants. The music, typically accompanied by dance and gatherings in hilly towns near harvests or weddings, was performed in a space where dancing was not typical. By simply re-situating a communal musical performance, belonging and the performativity of bodies in public space was explored. Projections of homogenous Pakhtun culture were challenged and the layered nature of public spaces by class, ethnicity and security were revealed. We were left with questions about the boundaries of privilege, subversion (as more than singular opposition), and the ability to alter an alien space by using familiar sound.

Winner of the juror’s prize award at the Mohawk Hudson Regional Exhibition, University Art Museum, UAlbany. 2012. Selected for screening at the Syracuse International Film Festival 2012, CityPossible Film Festival III, Asia Research Institute NUS Singapore 2014, Karachi Conference Film Festival 2014 and Digital Marrakech Festival 4th edition Arab Media Lab Foundation 2014. Semi-finalist for Institute for Public Art’s 2nd International Award for Public Art 2015.
