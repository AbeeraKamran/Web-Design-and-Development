Abu Omar is waiting for you* is a project by artists Afshin Dehkordi and Saeed Taji Farouky in collaboration with a group of young, UK-based refugees. The project explores the ways in which the threat and loss experienced by refugees can give rise to trauma, and how this trauma can be communicated and transformed.

What happens to this trauma? Is it present in the body? Does it become mythologised? How can it be manifested and what can be used as a coping strategy? What happens if we re-frame the trauma as a story, object or process? Does it heal? How is the experience of life changed by the experience of threat?

The project is based on an in-depth and long-term collaboration with around 10 young, UK-based refugees and asylum seekers. It began in July of 2015 and will have its first manifestation as a printmaking installation held during Refugee Week in London’s Royal Festival Hall. Following, there will be a symposium exploring psychiatry and art, as well as a free multilingual publication documenting the project.

The project is informed by several texts, including the Rise Manifesto, The Gift essay and the historical feminist essay The Tyranny of Structurelessness.

*The title is taken from a smuggler’s flyer found on a migrant’s Facebook group, where many networks exist to connect refugees with people smugglers.
